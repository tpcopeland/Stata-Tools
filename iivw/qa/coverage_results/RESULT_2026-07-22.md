## The `fiptiw` failure

It fails on **both** criteria: the Wilson interval [0.895, 0.930] excludes 0.95,
and the point estimate 0.914 is below the 0.92 hard floor.

**It is not a bias problem.** Bias is +0.01707 against an MCSE of 0.03918 —
under half an MCSE, i.e. indistinguishable from zero. The point estimator is
fine.

**It is a variance problem.** The bootstrap SE understates the true sampling
variability: mean SE 1.06195 against an empirical SD of 1.23911, a ratio of
**0.857** — the interval is ~14% too narrow, which is the right order of
magnitude to move coverage from 0.95 to 0.914.

The SE distribution is also right-skewed (median SE 0.98613 < mean SE 1.06195),
and `empSD = 1.239` is large relative to a truth of 1.0. That pattern is what
occasional extreme-weight replications look like, and Tompkins et al. 2025
identifies exactly that sensitivity for FIPTIW under weak positivity. **That is
a hypothesis consistent with the numbers, not a diagnosis** — nothing here
isolates the mechanism, and no run was made to test it.

## What this does and does not license

- The default refit bootstrap **is** coverage-validated at these settings for
  plain IIW and for point-treatment IPTW.
- It **is not** validated for FIPTIW. `e(iivw_inference_status)` must not report
  `cleared` for FIPTIW, and no user-facing surface may describe FIPTIW default
  inference as coverage-validated.
- One cell is one cell. This is a single correct-specification scenario per
  family at one sample size. SOL-04's own scope asks for misspecification arms,
  multiple sample sizes, and other link functions; none of that ran.

makes the interval too **wide**, so the fixed method should over-cover. Observed:

- `iiw`: SE ratio **1.0234**, fixed coverage 0.946 — same direction, mild.
- `fiptiw`: SE ratio **0.9994** — no separation at this cell.

In no family did the fixed method under-cover relative to refit, which is the
direction the framework said was impossible under correct specification.
